Nkata ndi Inyom Igbo — Website Rebuild

High-Level Plan (Client-Facing)

1. Vision

Build a new, secure, and user-friendly website that reflects Nkata ndi Inyom Igbo’s mission: empowering Igbo women, preserving culture, and strengthening community. The site will feel modern and trustworthy, and support members with a bookshelf, video library, blog, and other tools.

2. What We’re Building

Area Description
New foundation Full rebuild (no WordPress). Modern, fast, secure technology chosen for long-term reliability and ease of updates.
Bookshelf Digital library of books and resources. Members browse by category, search, and read in-browser where applicable. Access can be public for some items and members-only for others.
Video library Curated video library (cultural content, talks, events) powered by Mux. Members get a dedicated space to watch; playback is smooth and works well on mobile. We use Mux’s free tier to start (e.g. 10 videos, generous viewing minutes per month) and can scale as the library grows.
Blog Blog for stories, cultural pieces, leadership advice, event recaps, and member spotlights. Easy for your team to publish and update, with support for Igbo and English if desired.
Member experience Secure sign-up and login. Members get access to bookshelf and video library, and can see their own activity (e.g. saved or in-progress items) in a simple dashboard.
Additional features Event listings and RSVP, clear donation/membership calls-to-action, site-wide search, and mobile-friendly design. We aim for strong accessibility so the site is usable for as many people as possible.

3. Technology Choices (In Plain Terms)

  • Website platform: Next.js — fast, SEO-friendly, and well-suited for a content + member site.
  • Content management: A headless CMS (e.g. Strapi or Sanity) so your team can edit blog posts, book metadata, video descriptions, and events without touching code.
  • Video: Mux — professional streaming, reliable playback, and a free tier so we can launch the video library without upfront video hosting cost.
  • Hosting & database: Industry-standard hosting (e.g. Vercel) and a managed database (e.g. PostgreSQL via Supabase) for users, memberships, and app data.
  • Security: HTTPS everywhere, secure login, role-based access (e.g. public vs member), and no direct exposure of private media links.

4. Security & Privacy

  • All pages and forms served over HTTPS.
  • Secure authentication and optional two-factor login for admins.
  • Member-only areas (e.g. bookshelf, video library) protected so only logged-in members can access them.
  • Video and premium content delivered through secure, time-limited links where applicable so they can’t be easily shared.
  • Clear privacy and cookie practices, with room for a proper privacy policy and consent where required.

5. User Experience Goals

  • Clear and welcoming — Homepage and key pages tell your story and make it obvious how to join, donate, or get involved.
  • Easy to use — Simple navigation, search, and filters so visitors and members can find blog posts, books, and videos quickly.
  • Mobile-first — The site will be designed to work well on phones and tablets.
  • Inclusive — We’ll follow accessibility good practices so more people can use the site comfortably.
  • Fast and reliable — Quick load times and stable streaming so the site feels responsive and trustworthy.

6. Suggested Phases

Phase Focus Outcome
Phase 1 — Foundation New site structure, design system, CMS setup, and blog. Member sign-up and login. Basic member area. Live new site with blog and member access.
Phase 2 — Bookshelf Catalog structure, metadata in CMS, in-browser reading where applicable, and member-only access rules. Working bookshelf for members (and selected public items).
Phase 3 — Video library Mux integration, video upload and metadata, member-only video pages, and a simple “continue watching” or list view. Working video library for members.
Phase 4 — Enhancements Events/calendar, member dashboard refinements, donations/membership flows, search, and any remaining features. Full feature set and polish.

Phases can be adjusted (e.g. combine 2 and 3, or reorder) based on content readiness and priorities.

7. What We Need From You

  • Content: Final or draft text and structure for key pages (e.g. About, Offerings, Contact).
  • Media: Logos, images, and any existing video or book assets (or a list of what will be ready when).
  • Decisions: Who can edit the blog and catalog; whether some books/videos are public vs members-only; and any events or donation requirements.
  • Access: Domain and any existing accounts (e.g. email, social) we need to align with.

8. Summary

We will deliver a new, non-WordPress website that is secure, fast, and easy to use, with:

  • A blog for stories and updates
  • A bookshelf for digital resources
  • A video library powered by Mux (free tier to start)
  • Member-only access where needed
  • Mobile-friendly and accessible design
  • A clear path to add events, donations, and search in later phases
